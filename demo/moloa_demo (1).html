A Multi-Agent AI Approach to Loan Origination and Lifecycle Decisioning

MOLOA — Loan Origination
and Lifecycle Decisioning

A multi-agent AI system for two related decision domains: loan origination at intake, and lifecycle decisioning across the post-origination phases — early monitoring at month 6, and distress intervention at first 60+DPD. The acronym MOLOA stands for Multi-phase Origination, Lifecycle-aware Orchestration, and Adaptive coordination.
Substrate Freddie Mac SFLLD 2022 sample Sample 50,000 mortgages 1.82% default rate
Real Outputs · Trained Models

Trace a real Freddie Mac mortgage through MOLOA

Each loan was scored by the trained MOLOA models. Phase 1 outputs (LAR probability, agent gates, specialist scores) come directly from the trained PyTorch and XGBoost models. Phase 2 and Phase 3 reconstructions apply the same heuristics the trained models would compute for these inputs.
Architecture Layer

The agents and what they see

MOLOA uses non-overlapping specialist agents at each lifecycle phase. Every feature belongs to exactly one agent, so each agent is a domain-specific expert. A learned router (LAR) coordinates them.
01

Phase 1 — Origination Specialists

5 agents · Active at loan intake (month 0)
CreditAgent
Borrower trust

Evaluates the borrower's financial trustworthiness — the most direct credit-risk signal in mortgage origination.

Owns: CREDIT_SCORE · DTI · INTEREST_RATE · rate_spread · MIP

Strongest single specialist on this dataset (test AUC 0.764). FICO is the dominant signal in mortgage default and this agent owns it.

CollateralAgent
Property leverage

Evaluates the property and how leveraged the loan is against it. The secondary risk axis: even a strong credit profile turns risky on an over-leveraged loan.

Owns: LTV · OCLTV · PROPERTY_TYPE · OCCUPANCY · NUM_UNITS · payment_burden_estimate

Test AUC 0.580. Captures collateral fragility independently of borrower quality.

GeographicAgent
Spatial signal

Spatial / regional risk — same FICO and LTV behave differently in different states and metros.

Owns: STATE · MSA · POSTAL_CODE

Test AUC 0.537. Modest but non-trivial signal — captures regional housing-market dynamics.

ProductAgent
Loan structure

Evaluates the loan-product structure: balance, term, channel, amortization features, special programs.

Owns: ORIG_UPB · ORIG_LOAN_TERM · LOAN_PURPOSE · CHANNEL · AMORTIZATION_TYPE · PPM · INTEREST_ONLY_INDICATOR · SUPER_CONFORMING · PROGRAM · HARP_INDICATOR

Test AUC 0.565. Largest feature set; identifies non-standard loan products that carry elevated risk.

BorrowerAgent
Borrower profile

Borrower-profile features that don't fit cleanly into credit-score territory: first-time-buyer status, joint applicants, mortgage-insurance details.

Owns: NUM_BORROWERS · FIRST_TIME_HOMEBUYER · MI_CANCELLATION · PROPERTY_VALUATION_METHOD

Test AUC 0.619 — better than expected. First-time-homebuyer flag is genuinely predictive.

02

Phase 2 — Monitoring Specialists

3 agents · Active at month 6
TrajectoryAgent
Payment pattern

Reads the first six months of payment behavior to detect early distress signals — the freshest evidence available at month 6.

Owns: M6_MAX_DPD · M6_MEAN_DPD · M6_DPD_VOLATILITY · M6_PRINCIPAL_PAYDOWN

When this agent activates, MOLOA has caught a real signal Phase 1 couldn't see at intake. LAR-P2 routes 70%+ of attention to this agent when M6 distress is present.

EquityAgent
Equity drift

Tracks mark-to-market loan-to-value drift — is the borrower's equity position deteriorating?

Owns: LTV_AT_M6 · LTV_DRIFT · UPB_AT_M6 · original LTV · original OCLTV

Slow-moving signal, captures whether the property is appreciating or depreciating around the loan.

PriorDecisionAgent
Phase-1 carry-over

Carries Phase 1's accumulated risk view forward into Phase 2. The architectural bridge between phases.

Owns: phase1_lar_proba + the five Phase-1 specialist probabilities

When M6 is clean, LAR-P2 routes 88%+ to this agent — Phase 1's view dominates. When M6 distress appears, attention shifts to TrajectoryAgent. This is the lifecycle-aware coordination story.

03

Phase 3 — Causal-Inference Layer

Treatment-effect modeling · Triggered at first 60+DPD
InterventionAgent
T-learner + IPTW

For each distressed loan, estimates the conditional average treatment effect (CATE) of modification on cure probability. Recommends MODIFY · FORBEAR · SPECIALIST review.

Two underlying XGBoost models — one trained on modified loans, one on non-modified. CATE = P(cure | modify) − P(cure | no modify). Inverse-propensity-weighted to correct for severe selection bias (servicers don't randomly modify; propensity AUC = 0.94).

95% bootstrap CI on the population ATE: +25.3pp [+17.3, +31.7] cure-rate increase from modification. n=2,256 distressed, n=201 treated — pilot-study scale.

·

Coordination — Applied at Each Phase

LAR · CAN · AEL · the architectural backbone
LAR
Learned Agent Router

Attention-weighted gating across the specialist agents. A small PyTorch network maps (agent probabilities, agent confidences) to softmax-normalized routing weights. The final prediction is Σ gate_i × proba_i.

Critical training detail: entropy regularization (Shazeer et al. 2017, λ=0.05) on the batch-averaged gate distribution. Without it, LAR collapses to single-agent dominance under severe class imbalance. With it, gates distribute across all agents at 92% of maximum entropy.

CAN
Conflict Arbiter

Neural arbiter that takes agent reasoning vectors plus loan context and outputs a final binary decision — intended to resolve cases where specialists disagree.

Honest finding: CAN collapses to majority-class prediction under 1:54 imbalance (test AUC ≈ 0.50). Reported as a negative architectural result; the coordination role falls to LAR instead.

AEL
Active Escalation

Cost-sensitive deferral. Predicts P(MOLOA's auto-decision is wrong) and escalates to a human underwriter when the cost of being wrong exceeds the cost of human review.

Phase 1 calibration: auto-correct RM 200, auto-wrong RM 250,000, human review RM 1,500. Min-cost threshold: escalate 21.8% of cases (RM 26.6M total).

System Overview

Loan origination, extended through the lifecycle

The same coordination components are reapplied at each phase with phase-specific specialists.

The thesis claim is that a multi-agent coordination architecture designed for loan origination generalizes to lifecycle decisioning. The same components (LAR, CAN, AEL) are reapplied at three phases — origination at intake (Phase 1), early monitoring at month 6 (Phase 2), and distress intervention at first 60+DPD (Phase 3) — with phase-specific specialist agents.

01
Month 0

Origination Decisioning

Approve, deny, or escalate at loan intake. Five mortgage specialists coordinated by entropy-regularized LAR.
02
Month 6

Early Monitoring

Re-evaluation using payment trajectory. Three agents plus Cox proportional hazards for time-to-default.
03
First 60+DPD

Distress Intervention

Treatment-effect modeling. T-learner with propensity-score weighting. Recommends modify, forbear, or specialist review.

Honest scope

  • CAN failure mode. Conflict Arbitration Network collapses under 1:54 class imbalance (AUC 0.50–0.52). Reported as a negative architectural result.
  • n=201 treatment group. Phase 3 is a pilot study; per-loan CATE estimates have wide bootstrap CIs.
  • 2022 vintage. 3.5-year performance window; late-emerging defaults beyond month 24 are not captured.
  • No demographic fairness audit. Freddie Mac SFLLD does not publish race, sex, or age. Fairness analysis pivots to geographic and credit-band disparate impact.
